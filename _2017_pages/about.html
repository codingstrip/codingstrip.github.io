What?

"Can you write code that illustrates the above comics? Or guess at high-level what computing concepts were used in the comics?"

CodingStrip is a research project conducted by research team at the University of Waterloo. The starting point of this project is this question: "how can we leverage a medium of comics to teach computing concepts?"

The project is motivated by two main observations:

  • Comics is a fun, effective form of communication, especially for teaching abstract concepts.
  • Comics publications on computer science does not tie computing concepts back to code, preventing effective learning transfer.

Why?

Computing concepts are often introduced with abstract represetation, code. But the syntax complexities in code can (1) scare students, and (2) distract them from understanding a given concept.

How?

If you want to learn how you can design comics or use comics to teach computing concepts in your classroom, please come to our workshop!
